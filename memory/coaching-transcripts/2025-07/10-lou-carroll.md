# Coaching Call - Lou Carroll

**Date:** July 10, 2025  
**Duration:** ~55 minutes  
**Call Type:** New Client Onboarding  
**Energy/State:** Engaged, reflective  
**Tags:** #leadership #team-performance #banking #performance-management

## Client
Lou Carroll - Regional Director, Judo Bank (Orange, NSW)

## Key Topics Discussed

### 1. Personal Leadership Journey
- 21 years in banking (moved from NAB to Judo Bank)
- Came from technical banking background, promoted to leadership
- Biggest regret: Not asking questions early in leadership journey
- "You get so busy caught up in being in the weeds and on the tools and trying to problem solve for people"
- Engagement drops because you don't empower your team

### 2. Current Leadership Challenge
- 15 direct reports (spans geographic area: Hunter Valley to Cobar, Western Plains, Riverina, ACT, Snowys, South Coast)
- Previously managed smaller teams, now thrown into larger role
- Canberra team has performance issues
- Two people in particular: promised leadership roles, not performing, causing "whinge and bitching"
- High pay retention: "They pay them so well, they can't get a similar paid job anywhere else"

### 3. The "People Pleaser" Trap
- Struggles with engagement scores - takes them personally
- Over-indexes on keeping non-performers happy
- Recognizes he needs to be "harder" on performance
- Wants help having hard conversations

### 4. Solution: One-on-One Structure
**Three-Question Framework:**
1. What did you achieve in the last seven days?
2. What are you focusing on in the next seven days?
3. How can I support you to succeed?

**Cadence Recommendations:**
- Weekly performance one-on-ones (15-30 min max)
- Monthly development conversations
- Quarterly growth plans

**Key Insight:** "If you're just treating little things as big things... you're treating little things before they become big things"

### 5. Practical Implementation
- Start with Orange (where 2IC is strong) as pilot
- Identify 3-4 people to lead regions
- Reduce direct reports from 15 to more manageable 4-6
- "Get those people in their place and leading the team"
- Then cascade: they do one-on-ones with their reports

### 6. Judo Bank Context
- 6 years since getting banking licence, 10 years total
- ASX 200 listed (18 months)
- Built on "discretionary effort" - high expectations
- Culture of non-performance because no individual targets historically
- Now moving to pod-based targets

### 7. Personal Update
- Farm at Boronor (25km west of Orange)
- Three stepsons (12, 9, 5) and one own boy (12)
- Calving season
- Flying to Sydney next day

## Action Items

- [ ] Identify 3-4 potential leaders in team (by end of July)

- [ ] Start weekly one-on-ones with key people
- [ ] Have conversation with Canberra team about performance expectations

## Coaching Approach

- **Clarifying**: Helped Lou understand his challenge is structural, not personal
- **Structured**: Provided clear one-on-one framework with three questions
- **Strategic**: Suggested starting with Orange (proximity) as pilot
- **Validation**: Acknowledged his discomfort with hard conversations is common
- **Practical**: Broke down into smallest viable steps
