## Key Topics

### 1. Team Frustrations
- Frustration levels rising with team not showing initiative
- Guys turning up to work without being prepared
- Expectation: Come ready with runners list, actions completed
- Scott (team member) off - added workload pressure
- Another team member leaving soon - more pressure coming
- Realization: Needs to reset expectations, not lower them

### 2. High Expectations vs. Reality
- People need to feel like someone believes in them
- Challenge: Expectations too high = "holy cow" zone - can't see themselves getting there
- Solution: Break expectations down into smaller steps
- Paint the picture of end goal, but focus on first step
- Build confidence gradually

### 3. Delegation Framework
- Explained 4 stages of training/developing people:
  1. Directing - tell them exactly what to do
  2. Coaching - ask questions, still guide decisions
  3. Supporting - they're working independently, support available
  4. Delegating - they own it, come to you with problems
- Common mistake: Jump from directing to delegating = failure
- Current position: Between supporting and delegating
- Need observation check-points in place

### 4. Drug Court Reports Delegation
- Currently doing most drug court reports himself
- Scotty needs to take full ownership
- Solution: Standing meeting Thursday to review reports before Friday submission
- Gives 24 hours to fix any issues
- Creates accountability without micromanaging

### 5. Team Member Development
- Leaton and Blaine need to own the changeover process
- Chad stepping back, letting them have a go
- Need mid-week 5-minute catch-up: "How are you finding it? Anything you need?"
- Random observations to ensure they're on track

### 6. Supporting Joey Elliott
- Joey overwhelmed with emails
- Sat down with him, showed email management (folders, flags, to-do list)
- Taught him: flag emails → to-do list → star for priority → make a start
- Joey's transfer of care checklist - really good work
- Sometimes Joey asks the wrong people the wrong questions
- Need to remind him: not everyone knows everything

### 7. Transition Criteria Improvement
- Previously: Anyone could go to transition if they wanted
- Now: Clear criteria, first one knocked back recently
- Breath of fresh air to say "no" when criteria not met
- Now everyone has an exit date from the beginning
- Part of SMART goals - needs to be timely

### 8. Focus for Next Month (March)
- Continue building confidence in team
- Keep the focus on core activities
- Don't add new stuff to plates
- Solidify what's been implemented
- Look for improvements on existing processes
- Move to Quarter 2 with new initiatives
