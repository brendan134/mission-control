## Client
Steve Morris - Service Manager, Glen Innes Community Services

## Key Topics

### 1. Difficult Relationship with Joe (CEO)
- Joe has been "relentless" over past 3 weeks
- Staff appraisals triggered issues - Joe said things running smooth, next day everything challenged
- Steve considered leaving
- Joe's communication style: long emails at 3am, contradicts himself, can't let go of operational details
- Example: Joe emails about triage process for client issues already discussed and streamlined
- Joe checks shift reports from overseas, sends emails about things already being done
- Joe wants Steve at events but Steve values family time (Sunday nights)
- Joe called Steve out for not wanting to meet politicians - took him outside

### 2. Understanding Joe's Personality
- D-style (Dominant) personality
- Makes quick decisions, changes them often
- Moves on quickly after conflict
- Not emotionally aware
- Different to Steve's SC style (Steady/Conscientious)
- Not malicious, just very direct

### 3. Steve's Personality Profile (SC)
- Steady and Conscientious
- Needs time to weigh pros/cons before decisions
- Can come across as lazy or not caring (but isn't)
- Dislikes conflict, wants to be liked
- Prone to procrastination when unsure
- Makes decisions carefully, then sticks with them
- Background in addiction recovery - now feels safe and confident in who he is

### 4. Strategies for Managing Joe
- **Proactive communication**: Send updates before he asks
- **Seek to understand**: Ask questions to de-escalate
- Example: "Why is this so important to you?" instead of saying no
- Don't be fluffy - be direct but respectful
- Leverage strengths: thoughtful questions, listening skills

### 5. Personal Growth & Self-Awareness
- Knows his personality style well
- Confident in character today (post-recovery)
- Understanding that his style isn't lazy - it's deliberate
- Recognizes he doesn't want executive-level work, prefers hands-on
- Willing to challenge himself but on his terms

### 6. Development Progress
- Weekly performance check-ins: Doing well, missed one week due to leave
- 1:1s with team: Going great, helped understand how each person works
- Learning: As long as things get done proactively, that's what matters

### 7. Leave Coverage Plan
- Leaving for USA on 7th April
- Chad will cover case management
- Steve's caseload down to 3 clients - will distribute to team
- Need to have clear handover conversations with Chad and team
- Chad will do check-ins while Steve away

### 8. Staff Workshops
- Monthly workshops to upskill staff
- First two were great
- Third one (intake/admission) Joe sent 4-page email questioning why everyone needed to be there
- Steve forwarded Joe's previous email supporting the idea
- Joe didn't respond after that
- Next workshop already planned - transition topic
