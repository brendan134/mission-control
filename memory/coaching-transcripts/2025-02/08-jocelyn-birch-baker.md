# Coaching Call - Jocelyn Birch Baker

**Date:** Thursday, February 6, 2025
**Duration:** 1h 5m
**Call Type:** Business Coaching
**Energy/State:** Reflective, motivated, planning-focused
**Tags:** #veterinary #practice-sale #transition #revenue-streams #business-exit #lifestyle-change

## Client
Jocelyn Birch Baker - Veterinary Practice Owner

## Key Topics

### 1. Personal Context
- Living in rural area (Rockhampton), mango trees, peaceful location
- Working Friday-Sunday, about 30 hours total
- Has team: 12 people including nurses, receptionist, practice manager
- Staff retention good, especially mums returning to work
- Discussed staff member wanting 2 days per week - accommodated

### 2. Why Success Matters - Her "Why"
- Over the mental and physical load of the clinic
- Want to do something different
- Don't feel valued in clinic anymore - all clinical, running super
- Physical toll: 9-hour days, back sore, feet tired
- Wants new challenge, not clinical work
- Partner Ian - going through separation (also business partner)
- Complicated situation - need to separate both personal and business

### 3. Practice Sale Status
- Associate keen to buy - good friend for 7 years
- Contracts gone to lawyers, may reject some conditions
- Also talking to corporate and real estate agents
- Might have to repeat work if sale falls through
- Would accept slightly lower price to keep practice mother-friendly
- Should have got 2-3 quotes originally

### 4. Future Vision - The "What's Next"
- Four key revenue/income areas identified:
  1. **Easy Bet (Platform):** 50k target - consulting, webinars, mastermind
  2. **Pocket Products:** 20k target - selling products
  3. **Coaching:** 100k target - one-on-one coaching
  4. **Speaking/Networking:** 30k target

- Realistic assessment: Not highly confident about hitting these in 12 months
- Easy Bet product: 10 people at $X for Facebook group, monthly webinars
- Coaching at $1,700 per person, takes 2-3 days to onboard
- Need sales confidence - not good at getting people to come

### 5. Moving to Brisbane
- Unit purchase near family when practice sells
- Goal: new life, new friends, relevant conversations
- Limited social opportunities in Rocky
- Missing conversations about business, future, profession
- Wants to expand mind, meet like-minded people
- Toastmasters mentioned as interest
- Horses, horse riding - limited here but available in Brisbane

### 6. How to Measure Success
- "Laugh a lot more" - feels restricted in Rocky
- Deep conversations about business and future
- Meeting people who do "stuff"
- Freedom from mental load of current practice

## Action Items
- [ ] Review revenue targets and adjust if needed
- [ ] Develop Easy Bet product structure (mastermind group)
- [ ] Get clear on pricing for coaching offers
- [ ] Continue practice sale process
- [ ] Plan transition to Brisbane lifestyle

## Key Quotes
> "I don't think there's more I can actually do in the clinic but I can do more to reaching up the clinics."
> "Start with the end in mind."
> "I just don't roll about laughing but when I'm with the kids."
> "It's not them they've got nothing to talk about. It's my limit, not their limit."

- Validated the physical and mental toll
- Encouraged breaking down revenue targets to quarterly milestones
- Explored what success really looks like beyond numbers
- Discussed sales as providing value, not "being salesy"
