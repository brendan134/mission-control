## Context

First coaching call for the High Impact Leader Club. Review of the visual design and system structure for the new High Impact Leader program. Detailed discussion about presentation, modularity, and how to represent the system ecosystem visually.

---

## Key Topics Discussed

### Terminology Updates
- **SME Owner → Business Lens**: Changed from "owner" language to more universal "business lens" - applies to owners AND senior leaders in corporate
- **SME Leader → Leader Lens**: More inclusive of leadership roles beyond business ownership
- Makes sense for international audience (Philippines context)
- Need to update all slides and emblems with this change

### Visual Presentation Issues
- The "spiderweb" slide confused the test client
- Too much information, hard to digest
- Client suggested: hide that slide for now
- Alternative proposed: restructure to vertical arrangement (step-by-step)
- Each step should have its own block with related information underneath

### Ecosystem Concept
- Four elements need to work together as a system, not just a pathway
- **Foundation**: SME Impact Triangle (center)
- **Engine**: People Powered System
- **Implementation**: Seven Steps
- **Identity**: Five Stages of SME
- Challenge: How to visually represent this as an ecosystem
- Suggestion: Circular pattern with everything radiating outward from center

### Modularity & Lego Analogy
- Kriz suggested: treat visuals like Lego pieces or Transformers
- Each module fits together to create the whole
- Visual uniformity: currently different artwork styles for each component
- Need consistent look across all elements
- Spider web concept: center point with elements radiating outward

### Implementation Order
1. Start with High Impact Growth Pathway visual (the pathway itself)
2. Get that right first
3. Then integrate SME Impact Triangle
4. Then flow to other modules
- This is the "foundation" - everything flows from it

### Design Approach
- Kriz to draft first four layers of the circle:
  1. Impact Triangle (center)
  2. People Powered System
  3. Seven Steps
  4. Five Stages
- Use circular pattern radiating outward
- Spider web concept as visual framework

### Five Stages as Temperature Check
- Not necessarily a step on the pathway
- More like a thermometer to check where leader is at
- Designed to be taken multiple times to track progress
- Results can feed back into the system

### Cost Considerations
- Kriz concerned about revision costs
- Brendan: "We've got to slow down to go fast"
- Better to get it right now than have people not understand it
- "How do you sell a car with square tires?"
