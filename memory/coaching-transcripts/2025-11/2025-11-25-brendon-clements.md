## Context

Catch-up covering the aftermath of the 7-week strike at Bloomfield mining operations - the largest industrial dispute in recent memory. Brendon discusses lessons learned, team unity created, and forward strategy.

---

## Key Topics Discussed

### Strike Overview
- 270+ operators on picket line for 7 weeks
- Final deal: 17% over 4 years (vs original 16.75% offer)
- Lost 0.25% by striking - gained nothing
- Workers lost significant money (one worker lost $10K clear after overtime)
- Union rhetoric: "solidarity", "comrades" - but mathematically they lost

### The 40 Who Stayed (Core Team)
- 40 staff crossed picket line to keep operating
- Mix of OCEs (operators), maintenance, supervisors
- Learned to drive equipment (dump trucks, dozers, graders)
- Became highly united - "best seven weeks of my working life"
- Cooked barbues for team throughout (bacon and eggs, burgers, pizzas)
- Copped abuse on Facebook ("scabs", "low-life humans")

### What Happened
- Dan Rupert (Labor MP) visited picket line, gave rhetoric without facts
- Union paid strike pay (~2/3 wages) as "loan" - can come after workers if they cross picket
- Bikies and gang members involved in intimidation
- Internal politics harder to deal with than union itself
- Company management went from "let them stay out" to "pay them everything" in cycles

### Math Issue
- Union members didn't understand compounding interest
- Rejected 5-5-5-0 (15% with back pay) - "won't take deal with zero in it"
- Would reject 20% year 1 with zeros after - "would tell you to stick it"
- Brett (CEO) and Renata (HR) also confused by compounding
- Basic math year 6-7 level not understood

### Team Unity Created
- Working in trenches together = massive team building
- Supervisors who had issues with each other forgot them
- Collective goal: hold management accountable
- People asking for performance management training
- Toolbox talks driving truck exchanges, data on slow trucks
- Best possible outcome from worst possible situation

### Leadership Journey
- Brendon got on equipment himself - key decision
- "If I wasn't on it, I wasn't part of that" - needed to be in trenches
- Would not have made decision without prior coaching walks
- Luke (maintenance) was integral - would leave if Luke left
- Brad also critical to team

### Performance Focus Moving Forward
- Can't accept poor behaviour anymore
- Already drug-tested 2 people for sickies
- Performance management system being activated
- Keep momentum going - initial movement takes energy, now just maintain
- Focus points written down, bring all to one spot
- Measurable production increases coming

### Relationship with Brett (CEO)
- Brett wants to "rain hell" on unions
- Difference between rain hell vs nuclear missiles (self-destruction)
- Need to rebuild slowly, not carpet bomb
- Brett's moods inconsistent
- When Brett away, everything works fine
- Waiting for Brett to retire

### Culture Metrics
- Christmas party attendance: 20% (normally much higher)
- Best real perception survey
- Shows how unhappy staff are
- Won't do perception survey during dispute - data meaningless

###Brendan's Reflections
- Could have won if held out another 2 weeks
- Internal self-drama harder than union
- Would give anything to have CEO change
- Luke critical to operations
- Team of 40 (13%) is tipping point - 200 people under them
- Looking after those 40 = key strategy

### Where to End of Year
- No Christmas shutdown - forcing work
- Already written down focus points
- Keep momentum, keep feeding info
- Micro-stories from strike keep people motivated
- Use videos of abuse as motivation ("remind yourself why we do this")
- Not doing barbecues yet - too soon
