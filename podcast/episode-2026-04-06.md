## SECTION — THE 4 DECISION PATTERNS THAT KILL OWNERSHIP

Let me break down the four patterns that quietly train your team to wait:

### Pattern 1: The Reversal
You tell someone to make a call. They make it. Then you change your mind.
Every time you do that… you teach them: don’t decide, wait for me.

### Pattern 2: Escalation by Default
“Come to me with problems, not just solutions.”
Sounds reasonable. But it creates dependency.
Your team learns: if I push back, I’ll just have more work to do.
Easier to ask than to own it.

### Pattern 3: The Open Door Trap
“ My door is always open.”
What that actually communicates: come to me first.
Every conversation becomes your conversation.
Every decision becomes your decision.

### Pattern 4: Inconsistent Consequence
Sometimes you’re fine with their call.
Sometimes you’re not.
Your team can’t read your mind.
So they stop trying to read it… and just ask.
---

## SECTION — THE DECISION RIGHTS FRAMEWORK

This is where most leaders get stuck.
They know they need to delegate.
But they don’t know HOW to delegate decision-making.

There’s a simple ladder. Four levels:

**Level 1: Team decides**
Your team makes the call. You support, you don’t direct.
Used for: Day-to-day operational decisions

**Level 2: Team recommends, you approve**
They bring options, you pick one.
Used for: Decisions with financial impact or client risk

**Level 3: Decide together**
You collaborate on the decision.
Used for: Strategic choices that need your experience

**Level 4: Leader decides**
You make the call alone.
Used for: Vision, values, hiring, firing — the big ones

Most leaders are living in Level 2 and Level 3.
That’s where the chaos lives.

The fix: Be explicit about which level each decision sits at.

Walk through your last week.

- If yes → “Why did they bring it to me?”
- If they had clarity, would they have needed me?

If you can’t take a month off without the business slowing down…
That’s not a team problem.
That’s a design problem.

## PRACTICAL RESET
So here’s where to start.

Answer these three questions:
1. What decisions should my team own completely? (Level 1)
2. What decisions should they recommend, and I approve? (Level 2)
3. What decisions do I hold exclusively? (Level 4)

Start with ONE decision area.
Move it from Level 3 to Level 2.
See what happens.

## Episode Description
SEO-OPTIMISED PODCAST DESCRIPTION

In this episode of The High-Impact Leader Podcast, Brendan Rogers breaks down one of the most common leadership challenges: team members who constantly wait for decisions.

Most business owners and leaders assume the problem is their team — that their people don’t take enough ownership, don’t push enough, or aren’t confident enough to make calls.

But in reality, team decision-making stalls are almost never a people problem.

They’re a leadership design problem.

When your team consistently waits for you to decide, it’s usually because your decision-making structure has trained them to do exactly that. Without clear decision rights, explicit ownership levels, and consistent expectations, the safest choice for any team member is to ask the leader.

In this episode, Brendan unpacks the four decision patterns that quietly kill ownership in growing businesses:

1. The Reversal — when you say yes, then change your mind
2. Escalation by Default — when “bring me solutions, not problems” creates dependency
3. The Open Door Trap — when accessibility becomes a bottleneck
4. Inconsistent Consequence — when decisions are sometimes okay, sometimes not

He also introduces a practical Decision Rights Framework — a four-level ladder that makes explicit who decides what at every level of the business.

This episode is for leaders who feel like the bottleneck, who can’t take time off without everything slowing down, or who are tired of being the default decision-maker for every team question.

Because high-impact leadership isn’t about making more decisions.
It’s about designing a system where your team can make decisions without you.
