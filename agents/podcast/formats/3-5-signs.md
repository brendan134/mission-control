# Episode Format: 3 Signs / 5 Signs

**Format:** 3 Signs / 5 Signs
**Purpose:** Hybrid of Connection + Educational + Light Thought Reversal - help listener self-diagnose quickly, feel understood, move toward awareness
**Core Rule:** Do NOT turn each sign into a full lesson. Keep them sharp and recognisable.
**Goal:** The listener thinks: "That's me… I didn't even realise it."

---

## 3 Signs vs 5 Signs

- **3 Signs:** Deeper, more emotional, more space per sign
- **5 Signs:** Faster, punchier, more surface-level recognition

Choose based on how deep you want to go on each sign.

---

## Structure (10 Steps - LOCKED)

### 1. Opening Hook (0:00-0:45)
- Goal: Invite self-diagnosis
- **Examples:**
  - "Here are 3 signs you've become the bottleneck in your business…"
  - "If your team isn't performing, these 5 signs will tell you why…"

### 2. Standard Podcast Intro (0:45-1:30)
- Welcome listeners to the High-Impact Leader Podcast
- Brief value proposition
- Set up the episode topic

### 3. Context (1:30-2:30)
- Goal: Frame who this is for
- Keep it tight
- Make it specific to business owners/leaders

### 4. Sign #1 (2:30-5:00)
- **Structure:**
  - Name the sign
  - Describe behaviour
  - Give quick example

### 5. Sign #2 (5:00-7:30)
- Same structure as Sign #1
- Build deeper recognition

### 6. Sign #3 (7:30-10:00)
- Same structure
- This is often the strongest or most confronting
- (If using 5 signs, shorten each section slightly)

### 7. Sign #4 (if using 5 signs) (10:00-12:00)
- Shorter format
- Punchy recognition

### 8. Sign #5 (if using 5 signs) (12:00-13:30)
- Shorter format
- Final punch

*(Note: If 5 signs, compress timing accordingly)*

### 9. Micro-Insight / Pattern Recognition (after Sign #3 or #5)
- Goal: Tie the signs together
- **Example:** "If you're seeing yourself in these… this isn't random…"

### 10. What High-Impact Leaders Do (after Pattern Recognition)
- Goal: Identity contrast
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour-based
- No steps
- **Template:**
  - "High-Impact Leaders don't [common behaviour].
    They [different behaviour].
    They [identity-based way of operating]."

### 11. Light Direction (after What High-Impact Leaders Do)
- Goal: Point forward without over-teaching
- **Examples:**
  - "The shift starts with…"
  - "What needs to change is…"

### 12. Reflection Question
- Goal: Drive self-awareness
- Ask ONE question

## Delivery Rules (MANDATORY)

### Language
- Sharp and recognisable
- Short sentences
- No jargon
- Conversational tone

### DO NOT INCLUDE
- Turning each sign into a full lesson
- Over-explaining each sign
- Multiple ideas per sign

### MUST INCLUDE
- Clear, nameable signs
- Observable behaviours
- Real examples
- Pattern that ties them together

---

## "What High-Impact Leaders Do" Rules
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour only
- No "how"
- No frameworks
- Must contrast the signs

---

## Pacing Principles (10-13 Minutes)
- Spend more time on signs than explanation
- Keep context tight
- Let recognition build
- Light direction stays light

---

## Quality Control Check

### Recognition Check
1. Are the signs easily recognisable?
2. Does each sign create "that's me" moment?

### Structure Check
3. Is each sign consistent in structure?
4. Is the pattern tying them together clear?

### Discipline Check
5. Did we avoid turning signs into full lessons?
6. Did we keep them sharp?

### Identity Check
7. Does "What High-Impact Leaders Do" provide identity contrast?
8. Does light direction avoid teaching?

---

## One-Line Operating Principle

> "Signs help people see the problem before they're ready to solve it."
