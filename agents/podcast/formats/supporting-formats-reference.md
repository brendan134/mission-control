# Supporting Formats Reference

**Status:** Reference Document (Not Locked)
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-23

These formats support the core 5 locked formats. They provide variety while maintaining identity consistency through the "What High-Impact Leaders Do" section.

---

## 1. Mistakes Format

**Purpose:** Highlight common leadership mistakes with curiosity and authority

### Structure
- **Hook:** "3 mistakes leaders make…"
- **Mistake 1-3:** Each with description + why it happens
- **Why It Matters:** Connect mistakes to leadership identity
- **What High-Impact Leaders Do:** Identity contrast
- **Reflection:** One question

### Why It Works
- High curiosity
- Easy to consume
- Naturally leads into solutions

### Example Hooks
- "3 mistakes I see business owners make with their teams…"
- "The 5 mistakes that are keeping your team small…"

---

## 2. Myth vs Truth Format

**Purpose:** Challenge widely-held beliefs and replace with stronger truth

### Structure
- **Hook:** "The biggest myth about…"
- **Myth:** What people believe
- **Why People Believe It:** Cultural reinforcement
- **Why It's Wrong:** The flaw in thinking
- **Truth:** The better understanding
- **What High-Impact Leaders Do:** Identity contrast
- **Reflection:** One question

### Why It Works
- Strong authority positioning
- Great for Thought Reversal crossover

### Example Hooks
- "The biggest myth about delegation…"
- "Why the 'work harder' narrative is killing your business…"

---

## 3. Shift Format

**Purpose:** Show transformation through a key change moment

### Structure
- **Hook:** "The shift that changed everything…"
- **Old Way:** How things were
- **Breaking Point:** What forced the change
- **New Way:** What changed
- **What High-Impact Leaders Do:** Identity contrast
- **Reflection:** One question

### Why It Works
- Simple
- Powerful
- Identity-driven

### Example Hooks
- "The shift that took my leadership from chaos to clarity…"
- "The one shift that fixes team ownership…"

---

## 4. Before / After Format

**Purpose:** Show clear transformation from problem to desired state

### Structure
- **Hook:** "From [Point A] to [Point B]…"
- **Point A (Struggle):** Describe the problem state
- **Point B (Desired State):** Describe the outcome
- **What Changed:** The key shifts
- **What High-Impact Leaders Do:** Identity contrast
- **Reflection:** One question

### Why It Works
- Clear transformation
- Strong for ecosystem content

### Example Hooks
- "From bottleneck to building leaders…"
- "From doing everything to leading through design…"

---

## 5. One Problem Deep Dive

**Purpose:** Authority through deep focus on single problem

### Structure
- **Hook:** "The problem with [X]…"
- **One Problem Only:** Deep breakdown
- **Root Cause:** Why it persists
- **Light Direction:** Point forward without steps
- **What High-Impact Leaders Do:** Identity contrast
- **Reflection:** One question

### Why It Works
- Authority
- Depth
- Differentiation

### Example Hooks
- "The real reason your team isn't performing…"
- "Why decision-making keeps slowing you down…"

---

## 6. Pattern Recognition Format

**Purpose:** Position as high-level thinker through observed patterns

### Structure
- **Hook:** "I keep seeing this…"
- **Describe Pattern:** What you observe
- **Where It Shows Up:** Context and situations
- **Impact:** Cost of the pattern
- **What High-Impact Leaders Do:** Identity contrast
- **Reflection:** One question

### Why It Works
- Positions you as high-level thinker
- Strong connection + authority blend

### Example Hooks
- "I keep seeing this one pattern in scaling businesses…"
- "The pattern I see in leaders who burn out…"

---

## 7. Decision Frame Format

**Purpose:** Help leaders think through choices

### Structure
- **Hook:** "If you're deciding between X and Y…"
- **Break Down Option A:** Pros and cons
- **Break Down Option B:** Pros and cons
- **Clarify Thinking:** Which fits high-impact leadership
- **What High-Impact Leaders Do:** Identity contrast
- **Reflection:** One question

### Why It Works
- Practical
- Highly valuable
- Leadership-focused

### Example Hooks
- "Hiring vs outsourcing - what high-impact leaders choose…"
- "Centralised vs distributed decision-making…"

---

## Common Elements

All supporting formats include:

### "What High-Impact Leaders Do" Section
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour-based
- No frameworks
- Must contrast the format's theme

### Reflection Question
- One question only
- Drives self-awareness

### Soft CTA
- Natural next step
- Aligns with ecosystem

---

## One-Line Principle

> "Variety in format keeps attention. Consistency in identity builds authority."
