# Episode Format: Educational (What To Do)

**Format:** Educational (What To Do)
**Purpose:** Teach a leadership behaviour or decision process through structured explanation
**Core Rule:** Teach what to do, not everything you know
**Goal:** The listener thinks: "I can go and do this now."

---

## Structure (10 Steps - LOCKED)

### 1. Opening Hook (0:00-0:45)
- Goal: Call out the problem + introduce outcome
- Be direct
- State the problem clearly
- Hint at a simple solution
- **Formats:**
  - "If your team keeps [problem], here's what to do…"
  - "If you're struggling with [problem], this is the shift…"

### 2. Standard Podcast Intro (0:45-1:30)
- Welcome listeners to the High-Impact Leader Podcast
- Brief value proposition
- Set up the episode topic

### 3. Problem Clarity (1:30-3:00)
- Goal: Ensure the listener knows this is for them
- Describe the situation clearly
- Use observable behaviours
- Keep it tight (do not drift into full connection content)

### 4. Why This Happens (3:00-4:30)
- Goal: Provide simple insight (not deep theory)
- Give 1 core reason
- Keep it clear and simple
- Avoid over-explaining
- **Examples:**
  - "This usually happens because…"
  - "The reason this keeps showing up is…"

### 5. Transition To Action (4:30-5:00)
- Goal: Shift from problem → solution
- Set expectation clearly
- Reinforce simplicity
- **Example:** "Here's what to do instead…"

### 6. What To Do - Core Section (5:00-9:30)
- Goal: Provide clear, actionable steps
- **Structure:** 3 steps (preferred), 4 steps (max)

#### Step Format (For Each Step)
Each step must include:
1. **Clear Action** - What to do
2. **Short Explanation** - Why it works
3. **Example** - Real-world application

#### Step Delivery Rules
- Keep each step tight (60-90 seconds max)
- Use simple language
- Focus on behaviour, not theory
- Make it immediately usable

#### Example Structure
- **Step 1:** Clarify the decision ownership
  - Explain briefly
  - Give example
- **Step 2:** Set the expectation with your team
  - Explain briefly
  - Give example
- **Step 3:** Hold the boundary consistently
  - Explain briefly
  - Give example

### 7. What High-Impact Leaders Do (9:30-10:10)
- Goal: Reinforce identity behind the actions
- Connects action → identity
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour-based
- No frameworks
- No repetition of steps
- **Template:**
  - "High-Impact Leaders don't [common behaviour].
    They [different behaviour].
    They [identity-based way of operating]."

### 8. Implementation Simplification (10:10-11:30)
- Goal: Make execution feel easy
- Reduce overwhelm
- Reinforce simplicity
- Highlight starting point
- **Examples:**
  - "Start with step one…"
  - "You don't need to do all of this at once…"

### 9. Common Mistake To Avoid (11:30-12:30)
- Goal: Protect execution
- Call out 1 mistake
- Keep it simple
- Tie it back to the problem
- **Example:** "The mistake most people make here is…"

### 10. Reflection Question (12:30-13:15)
- Goal: Drive self-awareness and action
- Ask ONE question
- Keep it direct
- **Examples:**
  - "Which of these are you not doing right now?"
  - "Where do you need to start?"

## Delivery Rules (MANDATORY)

### Language
- Simple
- Direct
- Short sentences
- No jargon
- No em dashes

### DO NOT INCLUDE
- Over-complicated frameworks
- Too many steps
- Deep theory
- Trying to teach everything

### MUST INCLUDE
- Clear actions
- Real examples
- Behaviour-based instruction
- Practical application

---

## Step Rules
- Max 3-4 steps
- Each step must be actionable
- Each step must include an example
- Each step must be easy to implement

---

## "What High-Impact Leaders Do" Rules
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour only
- No "how"
- No frameworks
- Must elevate identity
- Must not repeat the steps

---

## Pacing Principles (10-13 Minutes)
- Do not rush the steps
- Spend most time in the "What To Do" section
- Keep early sections tight
- Focus on clarity over volume

---

## Quality Control Check

### Clarity Check
1. Is the problem clear within 2 minutes?
2. Is the solution simple and focused?

### Action Check
3. Can the listener take action immediately?
4. Are the steps practical and clear?

### Discipline Check
5. Did we avoid over-teaching?
6. Did we limit to 3–4 steps only?

### Identity Check
7. Does "What High-Impact Leaders Do" reinforce identity?
8. Does it avoid repeating the steps?

### Structure Check
9. Is there exactly ONE reflection question?
10. Does the CTA feel natural?

---

## One-Line Operating Principle

> "Clarity creates action. Simplicity drives execution."

---

## Final Note

If the episode feels:
- Complicated
- Overloaded
- Too long in explanation

**You have broken the framework.**

Return to:
- Simple
- Clear
- Actionable
