# Episode Format: Thought Reversal

**Format:** Thought Reversal
**Purpose:** Challenge a false belief, create a pattern interrupt, replace it with a better way of thinking
**Core Rule:** Disrupt the belief → create tension → introduce a new truth
**Goal:** The listener thinks: "I've been thinking about this wrong."

---

## Three Types of Thought Reversal

### 1. OG Thought Reversal
Directly challenges a common belief.
- **Example:** "Working longer hours is the worst way to grow your business"
- **When to use:** When the belief is widely held and needs direct confrontation

### 2. Context Switch Thought Reversal
Starts normal, then flips unexpectedly.
- **Example:** "Here's how to improve your team performance… Actually, that's the wrong approach…"
- **When to use:** When you want to create surprise and catch listeners off guard

### 3. Self-Identity Thought Reversal
Challenges how the listener sees themselves.
- **Example:** "You're not overwhelmed because you have too much to do… You're overwhelmed because of how you're leading"
- **When to use:** When the issue is identity-deep and requires self-reflection

## Structure (9 Steps - LOCKED)

### 1. Opening Hook (0:00-0:45)
- Goal: Disrupt belief immediately
- State the reversal clearly
- Make it bold

### 2. Standard Podcast Intro (0:45-1:30)
- Welcome listeners to the High-Impact Leader Podcast
- Brief value proposition
- Set up the episode topic

### 3. Common Belief (1:30-3:00)
- Goal: Acknowledge current thinking
- Explain what people believe
- Show why it makes sense

### 4. Why It Fails (3:00-6:00)
- Goal: Break the belief
- Show flaws
- Use examples
- Highlight consequences

### 5. Amplify The Cost (6:00-7:30)
- Goal: Deepen the pain
- Show impact on leader, team, business
- Make it real

### 6. The New Truth (7:30-9:00)
- Goal: Introduce the better belief
- Keep it simple
- Clear contrast

### 7. What High-Impact Leaders Do (9:00-9:40)
- Goal: Anchor the new belief in identity
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour-based
- No frameworks
- **Template:**
  - "High-Impact Leaders don't [common behaviour].
    They [different behaviour].
    They [identity-based way of operating]."

### 8. Light Application (9:40-11:30)
- Goal: Show how it plays out
- Keep it high level
- No step-by-step

### 9. Reflection Question (11:30-12:30)
- Goal: Drive internal shift
- Ask ONE question
- Keep it direct

## Delivery Rules (MANDATORY)

### Language
- Bold but not aggressive
- Clear and direct
- Short sentences
- No jargon
- No em dashes

### DO NOT INCLUDE
- Teaching session
- List of strategies
- Multiple beliefs
- Over-explaining

### MUST INCLUDE
- One core belief challenged
- Clear new truth introduced
- Identity elevation

---

## "What High-Impact Leaders Do" Rules
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour only
- No "how"
- No frameworks
- Must connect to the new truth
- Must elevate identity

---

## Pacing Principles (10-13 Minutes)
- Do not rush the "Why It Fails" section
- Spend time building tension before the new truth
- Keep application high-level
- Let the belief shift land

---

## Quality Control Check

### Belief Check
1. Is the belief clearly stated?
2. Is the belief widely held?

### Disruption Check
3. Does the "Why It Fail" section break the belief?
4. Is the cost amplified enough?

### Truth Check
5. Is the new truth clear?
6. Is the contrast obvious?

### Identity Check
7. Does "What High-Impact Leaders Do" reinforce the new truth?
8. Does it avoid repeating steps?

### Structure Check
9. Is there exactly ONE reflection question?
10. Does the CTA feel natural?

---

## One-Line Operating Principle

> "Break the belief, then rebuild it better."

---

## Final Note

If the episode becomes:
- A teaching session
- A list of strategies

**You have lost the thought reversal.**

Stay in:
- Belief
- Tension
- Truth
