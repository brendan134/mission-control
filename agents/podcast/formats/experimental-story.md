# Episode Format: Experimental / Story

**Format:** Experimental / Story
**Purpose:** Teach through lived experience - build trust through authenticity, deliver insight without "teaching mode"
**Core Rule:** Show the lesson through the story. Do NOT over-explain the lesson.
**Goal:** The listener thinks: "I can see myself in that… and I get the lesson."

---

## Structure (9 Steps - LOCKED)

### 1. Opening Hook (0:00-0:45)
- Goal: Curiosity + intrigue
- Start in the middle of something
- Create tension or curiosity
- Avoid full context upfront
- **Formats:**
  - "I didn't expect this to happen…"
  - "This completely changed how I think about…"
  - "Something happened recently…"

### 2. Standard Podcast Intro (0:45-1:30)
- Welcome listeners to the High-Impact Leader Podcast
- Brief value proposition
- Set up the episode topic

### 3. Story Setup (1:30-3:00)
- Goal: Establish context
- Who was involved
- Where it happened
- What was at stake
- **Rule:** Keep it simple. Do not over-detail.

### 4. Build The Story (3:00-7:00)
- Goal: Create engagement and relatability
- **Structure:**
  - Situation
  - Challenge
  - Tension
- Let the story unfold naturally
- Focus on decisions, actions, reactions
- Use real language

### 5. Turning Point (7:00-8:30)
- Goal: Highlight the key moment
- What changed?
- What was realised?
- What became obvious?
- **Important:** Do NOT fully explain the lesson yet. Just highlight the moment.

### 6. Lesson / Insight (8:30-9:30)
- Goal: Extract the meaning (light touch)
- Keep it short
- One core insight only
- No frameworks
- **Examples:**
  - "That's when it clicked…"
  - "What I realised was…"

### 7. What High-Impact Leaders Do (9:30-10:10)
- Goal: Connect the story to leadership identity
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour-based
- No frameworks
- No repetition of the story
- **Template:**
  - "High-Impact Leaders don't [common behaviour].
    They [different behaviour].
    They [identity-based way of operating]."

### 8. Application To Listener (10:10-11:30)
- Goal: Bridge story → listener's world
- Show where this applies
- Keep it relatable
- No step-by-step teaching
- **Examples:**
  - "This shows up when…"
  - "You'll see this in your business when…"

### 9. Reflection Question (11:30-12:30)
- Goal: Drive personal insight
- Ask ONE question
- Keep it simple and direct

## Delivery Rules (MANDATORY)

### Language
- Conversational
- Simple
- Clear
- No jargon
- No em dashes

### DO NOT INCLUDE
- Over-analysis of the story
- Turning the story into a framework
- Multiple lessons
- Over-explaining the meaning

### MUST INCLUDE
- Real experience
- Clear tension
- A turning point
- One clear insight

---

## Story Rules
- Story must feel real and human
- Focus on decisions and behaviour
- Avoid unnecessary detail
- One story per episode

---

## "What High-Impact Leaders Do" Rules
- Max 3 sentences
- Behaviour only
- No "how"
- No frameworks
- Must connect to the lesson
- Must elevate identity

---

## Pacing Principles (10-13 Minutes)
- Spend most time in the story
- Do not rush the turning point
- Keep insight short
- Let the story do the work

---

## Quality Control Check

### Story Check
1. Does the story feel real and engaging?
2. Is there clear tension and a turning point?

### Discipline Check
3. Did we avoid over-explaining the lesson?
4. Did we stick to ONE core insight?

### Identity Check
5. Does "What High-Impact Leaders Do" connect clearly?
6. Does it avoid repeating the story?

### Structure Check
7. Is there exactly ONE reflection question?
8. Does the CTA feel natural?

---

## One-Line Operating Principle

> "The story teaches the lesson better than you explaining it ever could."

---

## Final Note

If you find yourself:
- Explaining the story too much
- Adding multiple lessons
- Turning it into steps

**You have broken the framework.**

Return to:
- Story
- Experience
- Insight
