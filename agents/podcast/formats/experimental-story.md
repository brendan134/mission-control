# Episode Format: Experimental / Story

**Format:** Experimental / Story
**Purpose:** Use narrative or scenario-based storytelling that still lands in structured leadership insight

---

## Structure (9 Steps)

### 1. HOOK (20-30 sec)
- Open with a scene
- "Imagine this..."
- Immediate visual/feeling

### 2. SETTING THE STAGE (1-2 min)
- Who is this about? (anonymised)
- What was the situation?
- What's at stake?

### 3. THE CHALLENGE (1-2 min)
- What problem surfaced?
- What made it difficult?
- The moment of realisation

### 4. THE ATTEMPT (1-2 min)
- What did they try first?
- Why didn't it work?
- The struggle

### 5. THE TURNING POINT (2 min)
- What changed the approach?
- What insight emerged?
- The structural shift

### 6. THE NEW APPROACH (2-3 min)
- What did they do differently?
- The framework in action
- Specific changes made

### 7. THE RESULTS (1-2 min)
- Measurable outcomes
- What improved?
- How the team felt

### 8. EXTRACTING THE LESSON (1-2 min)
- What's the leadership principle?
- How can others apply this?
- The transferable insight

### 9. CLOSE
- Key takeaway
- Soft CTA
- Signature sign-off

---

## Story Structure Principles

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Specific** | Vivid details, not generic |
| **Anonymised** | Never identify client |
| **Permission-granting** | "You might relate to this" |
| **Structural insight** | Story serves framework |
| **Emotional truth** | Feel before lesson |

---

## Example: "The Founder Who Couldn't Let Go"

**Character:** Business owner, 15 employees, service business
**Challenge:** Can't delegate, doing everything
**Attempt:** Tried to delegate - failed, took back over
**Turning Point:** Realised not about trust, about system design
**New Approach:** Documented processes, clear decision rights
**Results:** 40% more capacity, team grew to 25

---

## Pre-Write Questions (Must Answer)

1. Who is the story about? (anonymised)
2. What's the central challenge?
3. What was the attempt that didn't work?
4. What insight turned it around?
5. What are the concrete results?
6. What's the transferable lesson?
7. What primary keyword anchors this?
8. What secondary keywords support it?
9. Primary objection: "Stories don't teach" - address

---

## Validation Checklist

- [ ] Scene-setting hook
- [ ] Clear protagonist
- [ ] Challenge defined
- [ ] Struggle shown
- [ ] Turning point clear
- [ ] Results quantified
- [ ] Lesson extracted
- [ ] Both objections addressed
- [ ] Primary keyword used
- [ ] Length: 10-13 min
- [ ] Tone: quiet authority
- [ ] Confidentiality maintained

---

## Confidentiality Rules

- ❌ No real names
- ❌ No company names
- ❌ No specific locations
- ❌ No identifiable details
- ✅ Changed industry
- ✅ Combined multiple clients
- ✅ "This is a pattern I see..."
